Research Publication

Implementation and Analysis of Parallel Prefix Adders using FPGA

Second International Conference on “Computational and Intelligent Techniques for Automation of Engineering Systems” (CITAES’22) · Jul 29, 2022

Show Publication Show on LinkedIn

This research presents an in-depth design and analysis of Parallel Prefix Adders (PPAs) implemented using FPGA, comparing architectures such as Ladner Fischer, Kogge Stone, Spanning Tree, and Brent Kung. The work evaluates power consumption, delay, and FPGA utilization using Xilinx ISE to identify optimal adder designs.

Recognized for its contributions to FPGA-based computing and optimization, this project received the Best Paper Award at the CITAES’22 conference.

FPGA Verilog VLSI / PPA Xilinx ISE Hardware Optimization Digital Adders Power Analysis Delay Estimation

Best Paper Award – CITAES’22

Issued by Seshadri Rao Gudlavalleru Engineering College · Jul 2022
Associated with Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University, Kakinada

This award was presented to Sappa Manoharsha for outstanding research and presentation of “Implementation and Analysis of Parallel Prefix Adders using FPGA” at the CITAES’22 conference.

The work demonstrated strong innovation in optimizing adder architectures for power, delay, and hardware utilization, leading to its selection as the conference’s Best Paper.
